The Problem

A World Not Built for Everyone

Visual impairment affects over 253 million people worldwide — yet the tools available to them have changed little in decades. The traditional white cane is effective, but fundamentally limited.

It only detects what is directly underfoot. A chair at chest height, a glass door, a staircase ahead — these remain invisible until it's too late. The problem is not just vision loss. It is the loss of safety, confidence, and independence.

Existing tech solutions require visual screen interaction, sighted setup, or constant manual operation — making them impractical for daily independent use.

Our Solution

Introducing Navii

A wearable AI system that transforms passive navigation into an informed, confident, and fully independent experience.

Feature 01
Multi-Level Obstacle Detection
Ultrasonic sensors detect obstacles at ground, waist, and head levels within a 3-meter range. Haptic feedback intensity increases as hazards get closer.
Feature 02
AI Object Classification
YOLO-based on-device AI classifies objects in real time — telling the user not just "something is there" but "a chair is ahead" or "a person is approaching."
Feature 03
OCR Text Reading
Points the camera at any printed text — signs, labels, menus — and reads it aloud instantly. Dramatically expands independence in public spaces.
Feature 04
Hands-Free & Screen-Free
No phone. No screen. No buttons. Navii works automatically from the moment it is worn — fully passive, always on.
Feature 05
Audio + Haptic Feedback
Bone conduction audio keeps ears unobstructed while delivering clear spoken descriptions. Vibration motors provide a secondary channel in noisy environments.
Feature 06
Lightweight Wearable Design
Housed in a glasses frame with an integrated rechargeable battery. Built for all-day comfort — portability was a constraint, not an afterthought.
How It Works

From Scene to Signal

A continuous real-time loop that starts the moment the glasses are worn — and never stops.

01
Capture
The Environment is Scanned
The camera captures continuous video frames while ultrasonic sensors emit pulses to measure the distance to nearby objects in real time.
02
Analyze
AI Processes the Scene
The onboard AI processor runs lightweight computer vision models to detect obstacles, identify object types, and extract printed text from the captured frames.
03
Decide
Relevance is Determined
The system evaluates proximity, object type, and context — filtering noise and determining exactly what information the user needs at that moment.
04
Communicate
The User is Alerted
Immediate feedback is delivered: a spoken audio description of the environment, or a haptic vibration pattern indicating obstacle proximity and severity.
05
Loop
The Cycle Repeats Instantly
The entire pipeline repeats continuously, adapting to the user's changing environment with zero manual input required — ever.

Roadmap

What's Next for Navii

The prototype proves the concept. The roadmap takes it to a real-world product.

▶ High Priority
Arabic Language Support
Full Arabic TTS output and OCR text recognition — making Navii accessible to 14M+ visually impaired individuals in the Arab world.
▶ High Priority
ESP32 Integration
Migrating to an ESP32-based architecture to dramatically reduce device size, power consumption, and production cost — a critical step toward mass production.
▶ Core Enhancement
Cloud-Assisted AI
Optional cloud inference for improved accuracy in complex environments, while maintaining full on-device fallback for offline use.
▶ Core Enhancement
Indoor Navigation & GPS
IMU and GPS integration for turn-by-turn audio navigation within campuses, offices, hospitals, and familiar environments.
▶ Core Enhancement
Expanded Language Support
Beyond Arabic — adding French and Urdu to serve larger global communities of visually impaired users across different regions.
▶ Core Enhancement
Companion Mobile App
An optional app for caregivers to configure preferences, update AI models, and review activity — without requiring any interaction from the user.
